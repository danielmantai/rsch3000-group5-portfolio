Literature Review

Evidence-based framing of identification barriers, service fragmentation, and housing-first approaches.

Pre-submit task: APA reference sweep. Jacob flagged this section in his draft. Daniel consolidates all citations into the References page before publish.

What the literature shows

Research consistently shows that individuals experiencing homelessness face structural barriers when accessing services. Lack of government-issued identification can restrict access to housing supports, employment programs, healthcare, and income assistance. In addition, many support systems operate independently, creating fragmented pathways that require individuals to repeatedly restart intake processes.

Studies on coordinated support models and Housing First approaches suggest that stable housing and integrated supports can improve outcomes. However, identity and documentation barriers remain under-addressed. This creates a gap where available programs may exist, but individuals cannot access them efficiently.

ESN Connect was developed in response to this gap by creating identity through verified service engagement rather than requiring formal identification upfront.
