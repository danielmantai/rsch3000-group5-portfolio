Group 5 Final Portfolio: ESN Connect

A shelter-issued identity and coordination system for Edmonton, built around the structural barrier that traps people who lack government-issued ID.

About this portfolio

ESN Connect is Group 5's final project for RSCH3000. It proposes a shelter-issued identity credential, backed by a shared digital record, that lets frontline workers vouch for a person and coordinate services across organizations without first requiring government-issued photo ID. The centrepiece is a working worker-facing prototype, wrapped in the systems analysis and evidence that led us to it.

We arrived at the problem through the systemic design work done in Weeks 3 through 9. An iceberg analysis, a causal loop diagram, and a stakeholder interview with Matt, a former outreach worker at Hope Mission Edmonton, all pointed at the same concrete barrier inside a much larger pattern of service fragmentation: without a photo ID, most services beyond emergency shelter cannot open a file, make a referral, or satisfy the documentation that their funding depends on. Identification sits at the entry point of nearly every pathway out of homelessness, and the current system offers no stable way to rebuild it once it is lost.

The portfolio is written for Dr. McNabb as the RSCH3000 instructor, and for three non-academic audiences: MLA housing opposition critics, the Mayor's office, and the frontline workers we interviewed.
