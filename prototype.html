Chapter 04

Prototype: ESNConnect

A working worker-facing portal for issuing and looking up shelter-network ID. Hosted on GitHub Pages. The ID card at the centre of the whole project.

What it is

ESN Connect is a prototype for a digital service coordination platform tied to the Edmonton Shelter Network ID card system. The prototype demonstrates what a functional shelter worker portal would look like if the ESN ID card were adopted at scale.

Who it is for: shelter staff and frontline social service workers. This is a worker-facing tool. Unhoused people do not interact with this system directly. Workers use it to create client records, look up existing files, and coordinate referrals across participating organizations.

What problem it solves: when someone experiencing homelessness arrives at a shelter without government-issued ID, workers cannot open a file, make referrals, or document service delivery in a way that satisfies government funding requirements. The ESN card breaks this cycle by creating a shelter-issued ID backed by a shared digital record. Workers at any participating organization can look up a client's history, add to their record, and make referrals, without requiring the client to re-prove their identity each time.

The funding problem

The real barrier is not that shelters refuse to cooperate or that workers don't want to help. The barrier is structural: government funding requires documented proof of service delivery tied to ID verification. Shelters can only receive funding for people they can demonstrably prove they helped, and that proof requires a verifiable identity.

Without ID, the person cannot be entered into a funding-reportable case file. Services provided are "off the books" for funding purposes. The organization absorbs the cost with no reimbursement. Referrals to other funded organizations cannot be officially logged. This traps unhoused people without ID entirely outside the formal service system.

The funding model needs to change so that shelter staff can vouch for unhoused people to access services even without government-issued ID. The ESN card is one mechanism for this: a network-issued credential that serves as proof of identity and service engagement within the shelter system. For this to work at scale, provincial funders and Service Alberta would need to accept the ESN record as a valid basis for funding claims.

Design rationale

The prototype is worker-facing rather than client-facing. Unhoused people arrive at a shelter with a phone, without a phone, intoxicated, sober, in crisis, or stable. A client-facing app assumes a lot. A worker portal that a trained staff member operates is more realistic. The worker is the trusted interface with the client.

Five pages, each tied to a function a frontline worker actually performs: login and auth guard; new-client intake with photo upload and generated ID card; client lookup with service history; an agency directory linking real Edmonton organizations; and a standalone funding-problem explainer that breaks the fourth wall on what this prototype demonstrates and what it deliberately does not.

The ESN ID card

The physical card is the tangible intervention at the centre of the project. Shelter staff issue a laminated card at first intake. A QR code on the card links to the digital profile. Workers at any participating organization can scan, add service-history notes, and coordinate referrals.

User journey

The storyboard below walks through a typical first contact: a client arrives at a shelter without ID, a worker issues the ESN card through the prototype, the record is accessible at the next organization the client visits, and the service history travels with the person rather than being recreated from scratch.

Prototype walkthrough

The live site has five pages. Each addresses a specific worker task.

1. Login / landing

Explains the system, describes who can access it, and presents a demo login form. Any non-empty credentials advance to the worker portal.

2. New client

Intake form with name, date of birth, photo upload, issuing shelter dropdown, and optional notes. On submit, the prototype generates a styled ID card display with a unique ESN identifier, issue date, issuing shelter, and a decorative QR placeholder.

3. Client lookup

Partial-name search against a small static demo database. Results display the client's card, expandable service history, and a placeholder "Add Referral" action.

4. Agency directory

Real Edmonton organizations tagged by service category: shelter, food, health, housing, employment, ID services. Each card links to the organization's website.

5. Funding problem explainer

A standalone page that breaks the fourth wall: this is a prototype, here is what it demonstrates, and here is the policy gap between this and something real.
