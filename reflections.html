Chapter 07

Reflections

Ethical considerations, AI use disclosure, and what the group learned from building ESN Connect.

Ethical considerations

Several ethical considerations emerged during this project:

  • Protection of personal data and privacy
  • Consent regarding information sharing
  • Avoiding surveillance concerns
  • Ensuring dignity and autonomy for users
  • Fair access across diverse communities

Our team recognized that any identity-based system must prioritize trust and user control in order to be effective. The same infrastructure that lets a person carry their service history across organizations could, in the wrong hands, become a tool of surveillance. Client control over who sees the record is a design requirement, not a feature to be added later.

AI use disclosure

Artificial intelligence tools were used to support brainstorming, drafting, editing, and organizing written content throughout the project. All AI-generated assistance was reviewed, revised, and verified by group members before inclusion. AI was used as a support tool rather than a replacement for original group thinking.

Specifically, AI was used to help synthesize systems-thinking artifacts, draft and revise written sections, generate the interactive prototype front-end, and organize this portfolio site. Research findings, stakeholder quotes, interview content, and decisions about scope and direction came from the group.

Group reflections and learnings

This project challenged our assumptions about homelessness and highlighted how many barriers are systemic rather than individual. A key learning was that identification requirements, fragmented services, and coordination failures can prevent people from accessing help even when services exist.

Our team also developed skills in systems thinking, empathy-based research, collaboration, and translating research insights into practical solutions. The interview with Matt McMechan reframed the problem for us; his observation that photo ID is "Ground Zero" for service access moved the whole project from abstract systems analysis to a concrete leverage point.
