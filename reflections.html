Chapter 07

Reflections

Ethical considerations, AI use disclosure, and what the group learned from building ESN Connect.

Group reflections and learnings

This project challenged our assumptions about homelessness and highlighted how many barriers are systemic rather than individual. A key learning was that identification requirements, fragmented services, and coordination failures can prevent people from accessing help even when services exist.

Our team developed skills in systems thinking, empathy-based research, collaboration, and translating research insights into practical solutions. The interview with Matt reframed the problem for us; his observation that photo ID is "Ground Zero" for service access moved the whole project from abstract systems analysis to a concrete leverage point.

Ethical considerations

Any identity-based system designed for a population already at risk carries weight. The same infrastructure that lets a person carry a service history across organizations can, in the wrong hands, become a tool of surveillance or exclusion. Our group treated client control as a design requirement from the start: consent to record creation, consent to cross-organization sharing, and the right to withdraw. Data governance has to sit outside any single shelter, with independent oversight.

Beyond data, the deeper ethics sit around consent and dignity. We interviewed Matt under a consent form that specified first-name attribution only, and we honoured that through the portfolio. The worker portal was designed around professional attestation instead of suspicion of the person in front of the worker. We kept the prototype deliberately limited: no real client data, no live backend, no pretense that software alone solves anything. The prototype makes the policy argument visible, and the policy argument is where the ethical stakes actually land.

AI use disclosure

Artificial intelligence tools were used to support brainstorming, drafting, editing, and organizing written content throughout the project. All AI-generated assistance was reviewed, revised, and verified by group members before inclusion. AI was used as a support tool alongside original group thinking.

Specifically, AI was used to help synthesize systems-thinking artifacts, draft and revise written sections, generate the interactive prototype front-end, and organize this portfolio site. Research findings, stakeholder quotes, interview content, and decisions about scope and direction came from the group.
